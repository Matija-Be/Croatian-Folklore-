Alpska

Dances

Alpska Zona is one of the more similer zone then the one we just saw. This zone is located on the far north of Croatia and it is also known to be one of the more festive zones similer to the one in the Panonska zone. This is actually seen as one of the more populer zones out of the four becuase of it's fast steps but not only that it goes more deeper in the story within the lyrics of this zone. Another intresting factor within this zone is that since it is located closer to the border of Hungary it has seen that there were elements from the huungarian dances that were incorperated from the Croatian dances. We have many dances from this region such as Zagorije, Pokuplka, Prigorije, Istre, Gradiscanskih Hrvata, Medimuja i Podravina, and so much more. One of the most most populer dances of this region is called Podravski Svati which is actually a wedding dance.

Looking into the tambura side even deeper it is also displayed how a lot of these dances incorperate violin sounds and violin is seen mostly in this specfic zone then in any other zone. Which really shows it's uniqness of this specfic zone becuase even though it is not required to have violin it is still wanted because of the history of our people and how back then people used violin for these specific dances.

Jadranska

Dances

Jadranska is seen as quite different than the other zones, the steps are no as heavy in there dances as it is in Dinarska but also the steps arent as fast pase as or so much music as Panonska and Alspka but rather their is seen as like for of a middle ground. A lot of these dances are from the places that are by the coast line and a lot of these dances are slower and they dont really require a lot of thought as they are more graceful and more relaxed on there feet. With how these dances are done it also reflect on how the people go throughout their day where there is no rush or no need to be strict with time becuase for them their is no rush for anything. The dances that are in this region are dances from Krka, Splita, Trogira, Kastela, Ciova, Braca and Hvara, Korcula and so much more.

Tambura

This idea of the dance being slow and gracefull is also expressed in the instrument side. We can see that there really is no rush when playing these song and it also shown in the way the performers play and one of the songs that really shows this style is called Dubrovacka Kontradansa
